HISTORY OF AMES CITY

Ames, Iowa, was established in 1864 as a vital station stop along the Cedar Rapids and Missouri Railroad. Founded by railroad magnate John Blair, the city was named in honor of U.S. Congressman Oakes Ames, a key figure in the construction of the transcontinental railroad. The city's history and growth have been profoundly shaped by the earlier 1858 establishment of the Iowa Agricultural College and Model Farm—now world-renowned as Iowa State University. Over the decades, Ames evolved from a modest midwestern transit hub into a vibrant center for agricultural research, higher education, and technological innovation.

Market Analysis




Pricing Based On Foundation Structure

Brick & Tile

Median Pricing $122,800.00


  • Era: Most commonly found in older, historic homes built before World War II.
  • Material: Constructed using fired clay bricks and hollow structural terracotta tiles.
  • Durability: Very sturdy, though the mortar can deteriorate and turn to dust over long periods.
  • Moisture Resistance: Highly porous; prone to moisture seepage and damp basements without proper exterior grading.
  • Maintenance: Requires periodic "repointing" (scraping out and replacing old mortar) to maintain structural integrity.
  • Aesthetics: Offers a classic, masonry appearance where the foundation is visible above grade.

Cinder Block

Median Pricing $140,000.00


  • Era: The standard for mid-century modern construction, highly popular from the 1950s through the 1970s.
  • Material: Built using stacked Concrete Masonry Units (CMUs), held together by mortar.
  • Strength: Excellent compressive strength to hold the house up, though weaker against lateral (sideways) soil pressure.
  • Vulnerabilities: The mortar joints create natural weak points where water intrusion or bowing can occur.
  • Insulation: The hollow cores of the blocks can be filled with concrete or insulating materials to improve thermal efficiency.
  • Cost: Provided a highly cost-effective and faster alternative to poured concrete during its peak usage.

Poured Concrete

Median Pricing $208,700.00


  • Era: The gold standard for modern home construction, dominating from the late 20th century to the present.
  • Material: Solid, continuous liquid concrete poured into forms, usually heavily reinforced with steel rebar.
  • Strength: Exceptional lateral and compressive strength; highly resistant to shifting soils and heavy earth pressure.
  • Moisture Resistance: The dense, seamless, joint-free construction minimizes entry points for water, making it highly waterproof.
  • Versatility: Can be easily adapted and poured into complex footprint shapes and varying depths.
  • Lifespan: Offers the best long-term durability, lowest maintenance, and excellent fire resistance.
